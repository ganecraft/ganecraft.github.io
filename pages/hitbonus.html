Hitbonus


Swords, axes and maces can receive hitbonus, increasing or decreasing their damage against mobs or players.

Generally one bonus is positive and the other is negative but it's possible for both to be positive when the bonus amount is low. The maximum is 75% (very low chance) and neither bonus can be 0%, both are always added/rolled. The bonus CAN NOT be transfered to other weapons, however, enchants from other weapons can still be transfered to the current one via greenbooks. When adding hitbonus to the weapon one of the bonuses is rolled to be the positive one and the other will be the amount of the positive one negated, halved and a few % randomly added or subtracted.

Applying


To add hitbonus you'll need a bonus adder, a trim template of any kind and the weapon you want to apply it to. The procedure can only be done inside of a smithing table.

The bonus values are applied after the smithing is completed. This is completely random and exact values can not be known beforehand.

If you didn't get the desired results you can always reroll the bonuses. There's no limit on how many times a certain item's hitbonus can be rerolled, but there is a time limit of 2 seconds between each roll.

After the bonuses are added/rerolled it can not be reversed.
