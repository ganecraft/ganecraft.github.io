### Hitbonus
<br>
<p>
Swords, axes and maces can receive hitbonus, increasing or decreasing their damage against mobs or players.
</p>
<div class="box-div">
<p class="box-text">
Generally one bonus is positive and the other is negative but it's possible for both to be positive when the bonus amount is low.
The maximum is <span class="mob-highlight">75%</span> (very low chance) and neither bonus can be <span class="mob-highlight">0%</span>, both are always added/rolled.
The bonus CAN NOT be transfered to other weapons, however, enchants from other weapons can still be transfered to the current one via <span class="mob-highlight">greenbooks</span>.
<img src="/assets/img/icons/knowledge_book.png">
When adding hitbonus to the weapon one of the bonuses is rolled to be the positive one and the other will be the amount of the positive one negated, halved and a few % randomly added or subtracted.
</p>
</div>

### Applying
<br>
<div class="box-div">
<p class="box-text">
To add hitbonus you'll need a bonus adder, a trim template of any kind and the weapon you want to apply it to.
<span class="mob-highlight">The procedure can only be done inside of a smithing table.</span>
</p>
</div>
<img src="/assets/img/hitbonus/upgrade_window.jpg">

<div class="box-div">
<p class="box-text">
The bonus values are applied after the smithing is completed.
This is completely random and exact values can not be known beforehand.
After the bonuses are added/rerolled it can not be reversed.
</p>
</div>
<img src="/assets/img/hitbonus/upgrade_window2.jpg">

<div class="box-div">
<p class="box-text">
If you didn't get the desired results you can always reroll the bonuses.
There's no limit on how many times a certain item's hitbonus can be rerolled, but there is a time limit of <span class="mob-highlight">2</span> seconds between each roll.
</p>
</div>
<img src="/assets/img/hitbonus/upgrade_result.jpg">
